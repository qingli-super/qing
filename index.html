Adoption and Learning in Enterprise Knowledge Systems

Using a randomized design and staged access, this project examines adoption and learning across two enterprise knowledge systems: Knowledge Base (KB), which converts unstructured interactions and documents into usable knowledge, and Knowledge Intelligence (KI), which codifies top performers’ practices into reusable organizational guidance.

Working Papers

Major revision · Management Science

Artificial Intelligence as a Relational Shock: Evidence on Newcomer–Seasoned Collaboration in Sales Organizations

with Jialie Chen and Danny Wang

Using a quasi-experiment and PSM–DiD, we show that workflow-embedded AI disproportionately increases seasoned salespeople’s workload and reduces their collaboration with newcomers. The loss of learning opportunities reveals a hidden organizational cost of AI adoption.

Invited to resubmit · Marketing Science

AI–Human Collaboration in Sequential Sales Funnels: Evidence from a Large-Scale Field Experiment

with Xiaolin Li, Danny Wang, and Flora Gu

In a randomized field experiment with nearly 100,000 prospects, we show that AI-initiated outreach nearly doubles the odds of loan application relative to a human-only workflow. The gains emerge during later human follow-up, as AI improves early information capture and segmentation and elicits stronger agent alignment.

Manuscript under preparation

Work Hard or Work Smart? Layered Generative AI and Salesperson Effort Allocation in Customer-Acquisition Operations

with Yijing Li, Albert Liu, Danny Wang, and Flora Gu

In a randomized field experiment, we compare a standard sales system, Summary AI, and Summary AI plus Diagnostic AI. Consistent with job demands–resources theory, Summary AI expands lead engagement, whereas Diagnostic AI redirects effort toward engagement quality without an additional sales gain; evidence on cognitive and metacognitive efficiency explains these distinct pathways.

Manuscript under preparation

Visual, Vocal, or Both? Decomposing the Value of Human Presence in AI-Generated Video Advertising

We separate the effects of AI-generated visual and vocal elements. AI-generated elements increase likes but reduce conversion and applicant quality, with vocal cues playing a larger role in conversion than visual cues.

Manuscript under preparation

When AI Crowds Out the Human Touch: A Randomized Field Experiment in Social Commerce Sales

with Jialie Chen and Danny Wang

Using a randomized field experiment in social commerce sales, this project examines how AI-driven outreach changes salespeople’s relational effort and customer conversion. It asks when AI complements human sales effort and when it displaces the relational interactions that support conversion.
